Prime targets for AI Agents

The goal is not just to understand the current process. It is to identify where AI Agents can reduce friction, remove manual effort, and generate measurable value.

Agent opportunity mapping

Using our scoring model — value × complexity × feasibility — we group tasks by where an Agent can create value fastest and safest.

Agent Ready

High value, clear decision logic, and clean data. These are the strongest candidates for immediate implementation.

Agent Assist

Useful opportunities where human judgement is still needed. These are ideal for human-in-the-loop workflows.

Agent Watch

Speculative or data-poor opportunities. These are parked for later until the process or data improves.

The output is a colour-coded heat map showing where an Agent will generate the fastest ROI and where effort should be delayed until risks or constraints are addressed.

This keeps decision-making commercial, practical, and grounded in feasibility rather than hype.

Role charter & success scorecard

We then facilitate a working session to convert opportunity into a controlled, measurable plan that your team can sign off with confidence.

Agent Role Charter — what the Agent will and will not do, with hand-off rules and escalation paths.

Success KPIs — speed, cost, and quality metrics, plus guardrails for bias, ethics, and uptime.

Data-readiness checklist — sources, owners, retention, and security requirements.

This charter prevents the scope creep and accountability gaps that derail many AI projects. It gives stakeholders a clearer operating model before development begins.

By the end of this stage, your team knows what success looks like, where the boundaries sit, and what needs to be true for implementation to work.

Frequently asked questions

These answers are written to remove friction, address board-level concerns, and make the next step easier.

What kinds of projects is this package best suited for?

This package is ideal where an AI Agent could replace or augment repetitive knowledge work — such as customer support, compliance checks, internal reporting, inventory coordination, or finance reconciliation.

Do you have a way I can assess our AI maturity?

Yes. We have a free AI maturity score checker you can use to assess how well your business has adopted AI compared with the opportunities available. Low scores are not unusual — this is a fast-moving field and most organisations still have a lot of ground to cover.

Who typically commissions this?

It is designed for operations leaders, digital transformation heads, CTOs, founders, and decision-makers who need a data-backed case for automation before committing major investment.

What exactly do we get?

You receive a workflow diagnosis, opportunity analysis, a drafted Agent Role Charter, a Success Scorecard template, and a costed implementation brief — plus the option of moving into build.

Is this a full development engagement?

It can be. Many clients proceed directly into implementation once the audit has clarified scope and priorities. Development is quoted separately once scope is agreed.

Can we skip the review and jump straight into implementation?

Yes. If you already have a clear specification or well-defined concept, we can move directly into development and translate your idea into prototypes and production-ready Agents.

How long does it take?

Most audits take 2–4 weeks from kick-off. If you are facing a deadline or board presentation, let us know and we will do our best to work around it.

What if we only have a rough idea?

That is normal. Our process is designed to turn vague goals into a scoped plan supported by workflow analysis, data-readiness checks, and success metrics.

What happens after the audit?

You receive a full audit report and delivery proposal. You can proceed with us or use the artefacts to brief another supplier. Either way, you leave with clarity and direction.

What exactly is an AI Agent?

An AI Agent is a task-focused automation tool that combines machine learning, business rules, and system integrations to carry out work on your behalf. Unlike a chatbot, it can monitor systems, make decisions, and take actions within defined limits.

How is an AI Agent different from ChatGPT or an LLM?

LLMs like ChatGPT generate content and answer questions. AI Agents go further: they are connected, persistent, goal-driven, and able to observe, decide, and act across systems such as CRMs, ERPs, inboxes, and APIs.

Can AI Agents really be trusted to do important tasks?

Yes — when designed properly. That is why we define clear boundaries, fallback rules, KPIs, and human-in-the-loop approval options where needed. Our aim is autonomy with accountability.
