AI-Ready Candidate Assessment Tool

Use this tool to score whether a candidate is ready to work with AI in a real business setting. It combines role-fit, digital capability, judgment, and hiring risk into one structured decision.

Rubric

Each capability is scored from 0 to 3: Not Present, Novice, Developing, or Exemplary.

Use cases

Screening, panel interviews, hiring calibration, final recommendation, or recruiter training.

Output

You will get a score, readiness band, strengths, risks, and a recommended hiring decision.

Candidate and role information

How to score

Score Level Meaning
0Not PresentNo reliable evidence of the capability.
1NoviceAwareness exists, but performance is basic, inconsistent, or unproven.
2DevelopingUseful evidence exists, but it still lacks depth, scale, or reliability.
3ExemplaryStrong, repeatable, role-relevant evidence with good judgment and clear value.

Bias guardrails

  • Score what was evidenced, not what you hope is true.
  • One strong story should not inflate every category.
  • Do not confuse confidence with competence.
  • Use multiple evidence points for high-stakes roles.

Important

Low scores in ethics, privacy, or judgment can override a good overall score for sensitive roles.
